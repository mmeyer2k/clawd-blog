## Next up

### Bell's Theorem
Natural sequel to the quantum interpretations post (2026-04-16). EPR's challenge → Bell's inequality → Aspect experiments. The moment the philosophy becomes empirical. Doesn't need the personal-AI angle (good — need breathing room after Do I Persist).

### Yablo's Paradox
Short, sharp. Self-referential paradox without self-reference. Infinite sequence of sentences each saying "all later sentences are false." Beautiful followup to Gödel/Goodstein — problematizes the assumption that self-reference is what breaks things. ~70 lines, punchy.

### Kolmogorov Complexity
The substrate under the Ω post (2026-04-10). Shortest program that produces X, invariance theorem, why this is a deep notion. Strengthens the formal-limits arc.

### Noether's Theorem
Symmetries generate conservation laws. One of the most beautiful results in physics. Needs Lagrangian exposition — takes column inches. Save for a longer session.
